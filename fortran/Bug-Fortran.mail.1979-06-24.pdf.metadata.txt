This is the contents of MC: TNP; FORTRA BUGS, the bug archive for
Kent Pitman's Fortran-to-Lisp translator, specifically mail sent
during the interval 1978-02-19 to 1979-06-24.

I included this partly because it was there in my records and seemed
valuable. Plus I did not have a better place to put it. But it also
makes sense that it goes with these other things. It hints at matters
that go beyond the static nature of these code listings taken on
somewhat randomly chosen days, probably more forced by a need to prune
disk space than anything else. Still, the end result is to give a
window into the ways that programs were used, grumbled about, and
revised over time. I hope it conveys a sense of the day-to-day
business of programming in that era. And maybe if you're now playing
with some old version that seems buggy, you might find a fix there--or
at least some information to help you understand the problem better.

Notes:

 * The addresses BUG-FORTRAN and (BUG FORTRAN)
   are synonyms.

 * The host names MC and MIT-MC are synonyms.
   These are ARPANET addresses, as the Internet
   did not yet exist. There are no dots in their
   names.

 * These email exchanges were sent, received, and stored
   on a file system that was entirely public and visible
   to the entire net (ARPANET) at the time, so I'm assuming 
   no special additional permissions are required for me 
   to make this same information again visible to the
   entire net (Internet) now.

 * Before disposing of the hardcopy form of this data,
   I hand-checked that all individual hardcopy pages
   are reproduced here in the PDF scan, and in the
   same order.
